# Problem, Personas, Journey

## Problem Statement

Early product teams often gather rich qualitative signal before they have a system for interpreting it. Notes are messy, inconsistent, and full of partial context. Teams need a way to turn that material into product decisions without pretending the AI knows more than the evidence supports.

## Why It Matters

The wrong early build decision can waste weeks. The right synthesis can reveal who hurts most, what job they are trying to complete, which workarounds prove urgency, and which experiment should happen next.

## Target Users

### Venture Studio Product Lead

Needs to compare opportunities across early concepts and coach teams toward sharper next experiments.

### Founder / Entrepreneur-In-Residence

Needs to turn conversations into a clearer problem narrative, MVP scope, and pilot success criteria.

### Staff/Founding Engineer

Needs enough structured signal to make smart technical tradeoffs without waiting for a polished product spec.

### Design Partner Manager

Needs to summarize feedback from design partners, identify recurring patterns, and track follow-up questions.

## Core Journey

1. Create a project for a product concept.
2. Paste messy synthetic notes from founder/customer/design-partner conversations.
3. Run extraction through the mock AI provider.
4. Review, edit, reject, or accept extracted insights.
5. View a dashboard summary of patterns and decision recommendations.
6. Decide build now, learn more, or ignore.

## Journey Success Criteria

- The user sees useful structure within minutes.
- The review workflow makes it obvious which insights are grounded.
- The dashboard makes the next product experiment easier to choose.
